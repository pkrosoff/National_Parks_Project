National Parks of the USA

Beginning with the founding of Yellowstone National Park in 1872, the National Parks system and subsequent National Park Service (founded 1916) now encompasses 62 national parks with millions of visitors each year. Below, you will find visualizations that explore the current parks system in great detail, explore the history of their growth, and help you plan a visit to a National Park!

Where Are Our National Parks?

The National Parks of the United States are sprinkled throughout our vast nation from sea to shining sea. Perhaps not surprisingly, in the large open areas in the Western half of the country, there is a denser cluster of parks with incredible landscapes. Hover your cursor over each star to unearth a couple more tidbits of information.

Park Visits through the Years

Each National Park has its own unique splendor and geography, as well as its own unique boundaries and size. Some parks are easy stops off the highway, others are colossal areas that require veritable odysseys just to find a trailhead which can have a significant effect on how many people are inclined to visit.
Of course, each park is also subject to the wiles of nature and natural events or disasters can render even the largest parks unsafe for visitation for a time.

Growth of the National Parks System

Beginning in 1903, when there were only six national parks (Yellowstone, Yosemite, Sequoia, Mt. Rainier, Crater Lake, Wind Cave), the map below travels over 116 years through 2019 showing the growth of the National Parks system. Each year, if a new park is established, a marker will appear in its location and for every park, circles will display and change size depending on the number of visits the park had in that year.
